A Golden Demonstration

Try resizing your browser or viewing this page on different devices. Use the icon in the upper right corner to see an overlay of the grid. It works by folding your screen sizes in half. Just like DIN paper sizes.

Trust me. You don't need rows.

You don't even need to worry about your columns. Everything is already in the column. Unless you really want more than 1 column.

But there's more than just 1 column size...

A two-way split

These two blocks of text will float side by side with some empty columns on the sides on large screens. On medium-sized screens the empty columns will disappear, and on small screens the blocks will be stacked vertically.

By the way

If you're viewing this page on an iOS device, it might zoom in wonkily when you rotate your device. This is because of a Mobile Safari bug.

Like me...

You might need more options for sizing your columns. Simply splitting them just won't cut it. You may want to try out my four-way splits.

My four-some

These 4 columns here will hold up splited on all screen sizes except the iPad mini and phone sizes. The whole folding in half idea means you can keep a golden ratio to all your columns sizes.

Which means

You can actually count on designing your site once and not worrying about display. Thus every folding will result in half the number of columns available.

But why?

The maximum layout width is based on 16 columns, only visible on big screens. Laptops will most likely use 8 columns, while phone and tablets will use only 4 columns at a time. Get it?
